The Shift

Hay un momento
en que todo
cambia.

There's a moment
when everything
shifts.

No es gradual. Lo sabes porque te acuerdas exactamente de cuándo fue. La primera vez que levantaste algo que juraste que no podías. La primera vez que tu cuerpo hizo lo que le pediste sin negociar. Algo se instaló ahí que no se va.

It's not gradual. You know because you remember exactly when it was. The first time you lifted something you swore you couldn't. The first time your body did what you asked without negotiating. Something settles in that moment that doesn't leave.

El cuerpo deja de ser el problema. Deja de ser la cosa que corriges, que escondes, que negocias en el espejo cada mañana. Se convierte en algo que construiste. Que funciona. Que hace lo que le pides — y se ve como el resultado de lo que hiciste, no como algo que te pasó.

Your body stops being the problem. It stops being the thing you fix, hide, negotiate with in the mirror every morning. It becomes something you built. Something that works. That does what you ask — and looks like the result of what you did, not something that happened to you.

Capable. Not as a concept. As something you've felt and can't unfeel.

Not a transformation.
This is just who you are now.
